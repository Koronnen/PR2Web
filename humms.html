Humanities and Social Science

The Humanities and Social Sciences (HUMSS) strand in senior high school focuses on providing a broad understanding of human behavior, society, culture, and the arts. It offers an interdisciplinary curriculum covering literature, philosophy, sociology, psychology, political science, and the arts. HUMSS aims to develop critical thinking, effective communication, and research skills, preparing students for higher education and careers in liberal arts, social sciences, education, and communication.




Below are the skills you will learn after taking this strand:


Skills Description
Critical Thinking

HUMSS cultivates the ability to analyze information critically, evaluate diverse perspectives, and make reasoned judgments.

Effective Communication

Students enhance their communication skills, both in written and oral forms, allowing them to express ideas clearly and persuasively.
Research and Analytical Skills

HUMSS emphasizes research, helping students develop strong analytical and research capabilities to gather and interpret information.

Empathy and Cultural Awareness The strand promotes understanding and empathy for diverse cultures, fostering cultural awareness and sensitivity.
Problem-Solving Students develop problem-solving skills by applying critical thinking and analysis to address real-world issues across various disciplines.
Ethical Reasoning HUMSS engages students in discussions on ethical considerations, promoting ethical reasoning in societal and individual contexts.
Interpersonal and Social Skills Through the study of human behavior and society, students enhance their interpersonal skills and gain a deeper understanding of social dynamics.
